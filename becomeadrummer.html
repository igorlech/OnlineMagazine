A SHORT INTRODUCTION TO ONE OF THE MOST POPULAR INSTRUMENTS IN THE WORLD

by Igor Lech

ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS OF LEARNING

It goes without saying that drums are not the easiest instrument to play. But learning it doesn’t have to be hard and boring – if you know the basic rules and follow them, you can easily become a quite skilful drummer. Playing will bring you joy and relaxation, not a frustration. But what should be done? Just follow this guide!

Be patient and eager! Learning how to drum takes some time and needs constant repeating.
Pay attention to the proper technique. By this, you will avoid bad and hurtful habits. One of the most important parts is holding the drumstick.
Get a master. If you don’t have any drum teacher in your area, make sure that you know what to do by watching tutorials, videos and playthroughs. It’s also a good idea to search for some online teachers or schools, like for example Drumeo. We prepared some basic tutorials here.
Use a metronome. Even the most experienced drummers do it while practising, as keeping rhythm stable is crucial for this instrument. This will allow you to get the sense of drumming and make your experience positive from the start.
Learn from the best. Be interested in music, listen and try to focus on drum parts in different songs. Try to imagine how to play them and be creative. Looking up to famous drummers might also be a great idea – they can be a huge inspiration and source of knowledge.
Have fun! Don’t forget that it’s important to simply enjoy playing the instrument. Try to be relaxed and don’t be mad when something goes wrong – these are the very first steps, it’s perfectly normal! It will also benefit your effectiveness while learning.

As you see, these few steps will help you develop yourself, as well as enjoy your drumming journey.

EVENTS AND WORKSHOPS

Besides learning to drum on your own or with private teacher, there are plenty of possibilities to do that with other people. These are some most known and famous drum festivals, where you can hear, watch and talk with experienced drummers, but also take part in lessons led by them. You can also always check if there are some local events nearby you.
